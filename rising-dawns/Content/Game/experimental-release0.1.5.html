Settle
Settle down, and gain the benefits and drawbacks of stationary life, for the rest of the game. Exclusive research is unlocked. Costs 600 food and 250 wood.

Pros: Increased worker resource production, less chance to be attacked by animals, less resources needed to build, and exclusive fortifications are unlocked.

Cons: Increased chance to be attacked by another civilization, increased chance for sickness, and increased resources needed to fortify.
Continue
Continue onward, and just keep going. You gain the benefits and drawbacks of nomadic life for the rest of the game. Exclusive research is unlocked. Costs 750 food.

Pros: Less resources needed to recruit people, less chance to be attacked by another civilization, less resources to fortify, increased raiding spoils, and exclusive buildings are unlocked.

Cons: Less fortifications available, increased chance to be attacked by an animal, more likely for a disease to kill a citizen, medicine is slower to unlock, and siege engines are never available.
Fire
Food intake is increased by 65%, and your chance to be attacked by animals is decreased, but chance to be attacked by another civilization is increased. Costs 350 wood, 300 stone, and 500 food.
Advanced Tools
Advanced tools such as hoes, shovels, and axes revolutionize the resource-gathering field. Increases resource gathering rate, and drastically decreases chance for lumberjacks to be eaten by wolves and hunters to be taken by barbarians. Costs 345 stone, 160 ore, and 750 food.
Shelter
Convert all tents to "Wooden Huts", which have 10 capacity compared to the tent's 5. Costs 340 food and 400 wood. Exclusive
Shelter
Convert all tents to "Heavy Tents", which have 7 capacity compared to the tent's 5. They are also much less likely to be blown away by a storm. Costs 340 wood and 500 food. Exclusive
Advanced Weapons
Advanced weapons made of stone further revolutionize the battlefield, and allow the training of "Early Spearmen", which--as the name suggests--are a very early heavy soldier with high damage and medium armor, but very high cost. Costs 320 wood, 175 stone, and 500 food. Exclusive
Better Clubs
Discover woods that can make longer and stronger clubs, which increase the attack of Clubbers, Spearmen, and Honorary Clubbers by 25 and their range by 2. Costs 400 wood and 350 food.
Agriculture
Discover farming, which allows the "Farmer" worker to be recruited for the first time. Farmers have less of a chance to be taken by barbarians than Hunters, collect more food, can collect Cotton (a special resource used for early armor, some trade, and clothing), and have limited defense possibilities, but they also require a building to be assigned to, and they cannot produce food during Winter. Costs 650 food, 400 stone, and 100 wood. Exclusive
Basic Agriculture
Start harvesting wild berries and shrubs, which allows hunters to gather more food, and gather some food (albeit reduced) during the winter. Costs 250 wood, 400 food, and 175 stone. Exclusive
Fortifications
Unlock the Fortification tab, which enables you to build walls and palisades of different types, a new research topic, outposts, and some pretty serious military and defense advancements. Costs 500 stone, 750 wood, and 350 food. Exclusive
Fortifications
Unlock the Fortification tab, which enables you to build palisades of different types, a new research topic, different types of outposts, and some interesting military advancements. Costs 250 stone, 800 wood, and 350 food. Exclusive
Irrigation
Discover irrigation, which improves farmer food gathering rate, and makes farms allowed to be placed even farm away from water. Costs 390 food, 500 wood, and 250 stone. Exclusive
Calendar
Develop a calendar, which puts the month system in place. Months give a little more organization to your civilization, and allows the formation of crop seasons and advanced growing techniques, which can increase food output tenfold. Costs 160 food, 300 stone, and 275 wood.
Ranged Weapons
Develop the first sling, which gives a huge early-game combat advantage against roaming barbarian raiders, and unlocks the "Slinger" soldier type. Costs 500 food, 350 wood, and 275 stone.
Expanded Storehouses
New building and refrigerating techniques are discovered, which means resource storage buildings' storage is doubled, and meat doesn't spoil for much longer than would be normal. New resource-storage building upgrades are now unlocked. Costs 350 wood, 250 stone, and 225 food.

Costs 650 food, 400 stone, and 100 wood. Exclusive
